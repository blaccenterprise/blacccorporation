B. Unincorporated Communities BC

Takeaway

The Importance of this module is to undertand the difference between an incorporated city and unincorporated commuunities.

Summary

An unincorporated community is a settlement not governed by its own local municipal corporation, but is administered as part of larger administrative divisions, such as a township, parish, borough, county, city, canton, state, province, or country. In other words, the State or a governmental entity with the same authority, dictates the interactions and engagements of unincorporated communities. Occasionally, municipalities dissolve or are owed to the creditor on the due dates, and services become the responsibility of a higher administration. Most other countries of the world either have no unincorporated area at all, or these are very rare, typically remote, outlying, sparsely populated or uninhabited areas. A ltst of unincorporated communities will be stationed on the right-side of the profile page. The heading of each tab will have the State, County and District, to help identify what communities are within the respective State's County's District.
